Learn the fundamental building blocks of English grammar.

Lesson 1.1: Parts of Speech

Nouns: Words that name people, places, things, or ideas.

Examples: book, teacher, school, happiness

Verbs: Words that show action or state of being.

Examples: run, eat, is, have

Adjectives: Words that describe nouns.

Examples: big, beautiful, happy, red
Practice: Identify the nouns, verbs, and adjectives in this sentence:
"The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog."

Lesson 1.2: Present Simple Tense

Use the present simple tense for habits, facts, and general truths.

Structure: Subject + Verb (+ s/es for he/she/it)

Examples:
  • I study English every day.
  • She works at a hospital.
  • They play soccer on weekends.
  • The sun rises in the east.
Practice: Complete the sentences:
  • My sister ___ (go) to school every morning.
  • We ___ (like) ice cream.
  • He ___ (play) the guitar.

Lesson 1.3: Articles (a, an, the)

A: Used before consonant sounds.

Examples: a book, a car, a university

An: Used before vowel sounds.

Examples: an apple, an hour, an umbrella

The: Used for specific things.

Examples: the book on the table, the sun, the first person

ðŸ“— Module 2: Essential Vocabulary

Learn common words for everyday communication.

Lesson 2.1: Family and Relationships

Family Members:
  • Mother/Mom - Female parent
  • Father/Dad - Male parent
  • Sister - Female sibling
  • Brother - Male sibling
  • Grandmother - Mother's or father's mother
  • Grandfather - Mother's or father's father
Practice: Write sentences about your family members using these words.

Lesson 2.2: Colors and Numbers

Basic Colors: red, blue, green, yellow, black, white, orange, purple, pink, brown

Numbers 1-20: one, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine, ten, eleven, twelve, thirteen, fourteen, fifteen, sixteen, seventeen, eighteen, nineteen, twenty

Lesson 2.3: Common Verbs

Everyday Actions:
  • eat - to consume food
  • drink - to consume liquid
  • walk - to move on foot
  • run - to move quickly
  • sleep - to rest at night
  • study - to learn
  • work - to do a job
  • play - to have fun

ðŸ“™ Module 3: Building Simple Sentences

Learn to construct clear and correct sentences.

Lesson 3.1: Subject + Verb + Object

Basic Sentence Structure:

  • Subject: Who/what does the action
  • Verb: The action
  • Object: Who/what receives the action

Examples:
  • I (subject) eat (verb) breakfast (object).
  • She (subject) reads (verb) books (object).
  • They (subject) play (verb) soccer (object).
Practice: Create 5 sentences using different subjects, verbs, and objects.

Lesson 3.2: Questions and Answers

Question Words:

  • What: What is your name?
  • Where: Where do you live?
  • When: When is your birthday?
  • Who: Who is your teacher?
  • Why: Why do you study English?
  • How: How are you?
