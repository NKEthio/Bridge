Lesson 1.1: Past Continuous and Past Perfect

Past Continuous: Actions in progress in the past.

Structure: was/were + verb-ing

Examples:
  • I was studying when you called.
  • They were playing soccer at 5 PM.
  • She was reading a book all afternoon.

Past Perfect: Actions completed before another past action.

Structure: had + past participle

Examples:
  • I had finished my homework before dinner.
  • She had already left when I arrived.
  • They had studied hard before the test.
Practice: Combine these actions using past continuous and past perfect:
1. You called me (past) / I was studying (in progress)
2. I arrived (past) / She left (earlier past)

Lesson 1.2: Conditional Sentences

First Conditional: Real possibility in the future.

If + present simple, will + base verb

Examples: If it rains, I will stay home.

Second Conditional: Unreal or unlikely situations.

If + past simple, would + base verb

Examples: If I were rich, I would travel the world.

Third Conditional: Unreal past situations.

If + past perfect, would have + past participle

Examples: If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam.

Lesson 1.3: Passive Voice

Passive Voice: When the action is more important than who does it.

Structure: be + past participle

Active vs Passive:
  • Active: The chef cooks the meal.
  • Passive: The meal is cooked by the chef.

  • Active: They built the house in 1990.
  • Passive: The house was built in 1990.
Practice: Convert to passive voice:
1. Shakespeare wrote Romeo and Juliet.
2. The company will launch a new product.

Learn words for academic and professional contexts.

Lesson 2.1: Academic Vocabulary

Common Academic Words:
  • Analyze: To examine in detail
  • Evaluate: To judge the value of something
  • Demonstrate: To show clearly
  • Illustrate: To explain with examples
  • Contrast: To show differences
  • Significant: Important or meaningful
  • Furthermore: In addition to
  • Consequently: As a result
Practice: Use three of these words in sentences about your studies.

Lesson 2.2: Phrasal Verbs

Phrasal Verbs: Verbs combined with prepositions or adverbs.

Common Phrasal Verbs:
  • Give up: To quit or stop trying
  • Look forward to: To anticipate with pleasure
  • Put off: To postpone
  • Turn down: To reject or refuse
  • Come across: To find by chance
  • Figure out: To understand or solve
  • Get along: To have a good relationship

Lesson 2.3: Collocations

Collocations: Words that often go together naturally.

Common Collocations:
  • Make a decision (not "do a decision")
  • Take a break (not "make a break")
  • Do homework (not "make homework")
  • Have a conversation (not "make a conversation")
  • Strong coffee (not "powerful coffee")
  • Heavy rain (not "strong rain")

Improve your ability to understand texts and express ideas clearly.

Lesson 3.1: Reading Strategies

Effective Reading Techniques:

  • Skimming: Quick reading to get the main idea
  • Scanning: Looking for specific information
  • Context Clues: Using surrounding words to understand unknown vocabulary
  • Making Inferences: Understanding implied meanings
Practice: Read a short article and identify:
1. The main idea
2. Supporting details
3. New vocabulary and their meanings from context

Lesson 3.2: Paragraph Writing

Paragraph Structure:

  • Topic Sentence: Main idea of the paragraph
  • Supporting Sentences: Details, examples, and explanations
  • Concluding Sentence: Summary or transition

Example Paragraph:
Regular exercise has numerous health benefits. First, it strengthens the cardiovascular system, reducing the risk of heart disease. Additionally, exercise helps maintain a healthy weight and improves mental well-being. Therefore, incorporating physical activity into daily routines is essential for overall health.
Practice: Write a paragraph about the importance of education using proper structure.

Lesson 3.3: Essay Structure

Five-Paragraph Essay:

  • Introduction: Hook, background, thesis statement
  • Body Paragraph 1: First main point with support
  • Body Paragraph 2: Second main point with support
  • Body Paragraph 3: Third main point with support
  • Conclusion: Restate thesis, summarize points, final thought
